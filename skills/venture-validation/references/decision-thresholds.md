# Decision Thresholds

Use these thresholds when deciding go, pivot, or kill.

## Go

Go when all of the following are substantially true:

1. Demand is strongly supported
   - users clearly describe the pain
   - they care enough to spend time, money, or attention
   - early conversion signals are not weak

2. Solution value is supported
   - users prefer the offer over the current workaround
   - they show real switching behavior or strong repeat usage

3. Business model has a plausible path
   - real payment has happened or the path to payment is clear
   - unit economics are positive or plausibly fixable soon

4. Growth has an early foothold
   - at least one acquisition path can repeatedly bring target users
   - the next-stage scale target looks feasible

Simple interpretation:
You found a small set of users who clearly care, a solution they meaningfully prefer, and an early loop that can become a business.

## Pivot

Pivot when some important truth is present, but the current design is wrong.

Typical signals:

- the pain is real, but the current user segment is wrong
- the user wants the result, but not this solution
- the product is valued, but the pricing or cost structure is broken
- people buy, but the acquisition method does not scale

Pivot by preserving the strongest validated piece and changing the weakest unproven piece.

## Kill

Kill when one of these is true:

1. Demand is weak after repeated honest testing
2. Users reject the value proposition relative to existing alternatives
3. Unit economics remain implausible after multiple serious attempts
4. Success requires capabilities or conditions the team cannot realistically obtain

Kill means the current thesis is not worth further resource burn.
It does not mean the founder is wrong forever.
